Vector Space

When solving systems of linear equations, we sometimes encounter the situation that there are more than one solution to the system.

For example, when solving this system of equations,

{1x+3y+4z=20x+2y+1z=12x+4y+7z=3\begin{cases} 1x + 3y +4z = 2\\ 0x + 2y +1z = 1\\ 2x + 4y +7z = 3 \end{cases} ⎩⎪⎨⎪⎧​1x+3y+4z=20x+2y+1z=12x+4y+7z=3​

Write it into argumented matrix,

[134202112473]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 4 & 2\\ 0 & 2 & 1 & 1\\ 2 & 4 & 7 & 3 \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​102​324​417​213​⎦⎤​

Use gauss-jordan elimination,

[105/21/2011/21/20000]\left[ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 5/2 & 1/2\\ 0 & 1 & 1/2 & 1/2\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix}\right] ⎣⎡​100​010​5/21/20​1/21/20​⎦⎤​

So that we get

{1x+5/2z=1/21y+1/2z=1/2\begin{cases} 1x \quad +5/2z = 1/2\\ \quad 1y +1/2z = 1/2 \end{cases} {1x+5/2z=1/21y+1/2z=1/2​

which the solution set to the system is

{x=1/2−5/2ty=1/2−1/2tz=t\begin{cases} x = 1/2 - 5/2t\\ y = 1/2 - 1/2t\\ z= t \end{cases} ⎩⎪⎨⎪⎧​x=1/2−5/2ty=1/2−1/2tz=t​
